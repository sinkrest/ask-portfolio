# Thought Leadership & Writing

Roman writes regularly about the intersection of AI and product management. His content voice is practitioner-first — sharing insights from building, not observing.

## Key Themes

### Context Engineering
The most misunderstood skill in AI product work. Most teams think "better prompts" means longer prompts. Roman argues the real skill is knowing what context to remove. A focused, well-structured prompt with the right 200 tokens beats a 2,000-token context dump. This insight comes directly from building production AI tools where reliability matters more than capability.

### The Full-Stack Builder PM
The PM role is evolving. Roman believes the most valuable PMs in the AI era are those who can build — not just specify. When LinkedIn killed its Associate PM program, it signalled a broader shift: companies want PMs who can prototype, test, and ship alongside engineers. Roman embodies this through his portfolio of self-built tools.

### Prompt Architecture as Product Thinking
Writing prompts is not engineering — it's product design. The same skills that make a good PRD (clarity, structure, user empathy, constraints) make a good system prompt. Roman's LinkedIn content explores this parallel, making prompt design accessible to PMs who see it as "too technical."

### Building in Public
Roman's "Roman Builds" series showcases one tool or workflow per post. The approach is deliberate: show the work, not the theory. Each post demonstrates a tool he built, explains the product problem it solves, and shares what he learned. No hashtags, no buzzwords, no engagement bait — just practitioner perspective.

## Writing Voice

- **Tone:** Peer-to-peer, conversational, direct
- **No:** Hashtags, emojis, engagement bait, AI hype
- **Yes:** Specific examples, real numbers, honest trade-offs
- **Format:** Short paragraphs, clear structure, one idea per post

## Key Takeaways

- Writes from practitioner experience, not observation — every post is grounded in something built
- Core thesis: PMs who build AI products will outperform PMs who only manage them
- Context engineering and prompt architecture are treated as product disciplines
- LinkedIn presence supports professional positioning as an AI-native product leader
