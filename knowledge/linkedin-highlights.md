# Thought Leadership & Writing

Roman writes regularly about the intersection of AI, product management, and manufacturing. His content voice is practitioner-first — sharing insights from building, not observing.

## Key Themes

### The Forkable Factory — Physical Products Should Be Developed The Way Software Is
Roman's flagship thesis. Manufacturing still operates like pre-git software development: one master drawing, one supplier relationship, one slow handoff loop. Software got better when it got version control, CI, and fast feedback. Roman argues the same transformation is now possible for physical products, powered by agentic environments and distributed manufacturing APIs. He publishes the research in public on romanmartins.com.

### Context Engineering
The most misunderstood skill in AI product work. Most teams think "better prompts" means longer prompts. Roman argues the real skill is knowing what context to remove. A focused, well-structured prompt with the right 200 tokens beats a 2,000-token context dump. This insight comes directly from building production AI tools where reliability matters more than capability — the same discipline a factory uses when deciding which parameters to log.

### The Full-Stack Builder PM
The PM role is evolving. Roman believes the most valuable PMs in the AI era are those who can build — not just specify. Companies want PMs who can prototype, test, and ship alongside engineers. Roman embodies this through his portfolio of self-built tools and the AI workflows he runs inside a real industrial OEM.

### Prompt Architecture as Product Thinking
Writing prompts is not engineering — it's product design. The same skills that make a good PRD (clarity, structure, user empathy, constraints) make a good system prompt. Roman's LinkedIn content explores this parallel, making prompt design accessible to PMs who see it as "too technical" and to industrial leaders who see it as "too soft."

### Building in Public
Roman's "Roman Builds" series showcases one tool, workflow, or build log per post. The approach is deliberate: show the work, not the theory. Each post demonstrates a tool he built, explains the product problem it solves, and shares what he learned. No hashtags, no buzzwords, no engagement bait — just practitioner perspective.

## Writing Voice

- **Tone:** Peer-to-peer, conversational, direct
- **No:** Hashtags, emojis, engagement bait, AI hype
- **Yes:** Specific examples, real numbers, honest trade-offs
- **Format:** Short paragraphs, clear structure, one idea per post

## Key Takeaways

- Writes from practitioner experience, not observation — every post is grounded in something built
- Core thesis: PMs who build AI products will outperform PMs who only manage them, and manufacturing will be transformed by people fluent in both worlds
- Context engineering, prompt architecture, and the Forkable Factory loop are the three recurring themes
- LinkedIn presence supports professional positioning as an AI-native product leader with deep industrial DNA
