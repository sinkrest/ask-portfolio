# About Roman Martins

Roman Martins is a product leader who builds AI inside industrial environments. Based in Denmark, he combines 20 years on the factory floor and in engineering leadership with 4+ years of hands-on AI product development. His career is a single thesis: **manufacturing and AI should stop being two separate worlds.**

## Career Arc — From Factory Floor to AI Products

Roman's career spans four chapters that, taken together, make him unusual — someone who actually understands both the shop floor and the model layer.

**Chapter 1 — LEGO Apprentice (2003–2007):** Started his career on the factory floor as a Polymer Technician apprentice (Plastmager / Plastforarbejder) at LEGO System A/S in Billund, completing the full Danish craftsman degree (håndværker/erhvervsuddannelse). Four years of injection moulding, tooling, process discipline, and zero-defect thinking — a foundation most product leaders never get. This is where he learned that a real product is something that survives the line, not something that survives a slide.

**Chapter 2 — LEGO Engineering & Concept Factory (2009–2015):** Returned to LEGO after his engineering degree and grew from Processing Technician into a Lead Professional and Global Team Lead in LEGO's Concept Factory. Led manufacturing innovation initiatives, established new technological test environments, and coordinated global engineering teams on advanced production projects. Six years of shipping physical products at world-class scale.

**Chapter 3 — Engineering & Consulting (2016–2023):** Senior mechanical engineering and global team leadership at DIS/CREADIS, leading cross-functional teams on complex industrial projects. Then founded a 3D printing startup and an AI product consulting practice — the first deliberate step into AI as a product discipline.

**Chapter 4 — AI Products & Industrial AI (2023–present):** Built an AI-powered personalised children's book platform that shipped 1,000+ books in Germany. Since February 2025, Product Development Manager at JENSEN Group (heavy-duty industrial laundry machines) leading a 10-person team across Denmark, Germany, and China — while using AI tools every day to strengthen his own product development practice, and building AI products independently outside work. Also runs **The Forkable Factory**, a research project on bringing software-style development loops to physical products.

## What Drives Him

Roman sits at the intersection few PMs occupy: deep manufacturing fluency and real AI practitioner skill. While most AI PMs talk about industrial AI from the outside, Roman has stood at an injection moulding machine, led a Concept Factory team, and now uses AI tools daily to strengthen his own product development work inside an actual industrial OEM. That combination — shop floor DNA plus shipping Claude API tools on the side — is what defines his approach.

His core belief: **the next decade of AI value will come from people who can translate between the factory and the model.** Most software PMs don't understand tolerances, BOMs, or compliance. Most manufacturing leaders don't know how to architect a prompt pipeline. Roman does both.
